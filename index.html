Our Solar System

An experiment with CSS animations & -webkit.

  • Sun

    The Sun

    The centerpiece of our solar system. Did you know that the Sun actually consists mostly of hydrogen and helium? The heat and light we recieve actually comes from the nuclear fusion that occurs inside the Sun, which releases huge amounts of energy. Its mass is so huge, that it makes up 99.8% of our entire solar system's mass.

    Mass 1.989 x 1030 kg
    Orbit Period 225-250 mil years (*Sgr A*)
    Radius 6.955 x 105 km (1 *R☉)
    Gravity 274.0 m/s2
    Temperature 5778 *K
    Moon(s) None
  • Mercury

    Mercury

    Being the closest planet to the Sun, its temperature can reach 430°C during the day and -173.15°C during the night. These long ranges occur because of its thin atmosphere, being essentially a vacuum because of its low gravity (38% of the Earth's) making it possible for solar waves to blow away whatever atmosphere it has left onto outer space.

    Mass 3.3022 x 1023 kg
    Orbit Period 88 days (Sun)
    Radius 2400 km (0.0035 *R☉)
    Gravity 3.7 m/s2
    Temperature Day: 700 *K Night: 100 *K
    Moon(s) None
  • Venus

    Venus

    Named after the Roman goddess of love and beauty, Venus is the second brightest object in the night sky after the moon. It's considered as Earth's sister planet because of their similarities when it comes to mass and radius.

    Mass 4.867 x 1024 kg
    Orbit Period 225 days (Sun)
    Radius 6052 km (0.0087 *R☉)
    Gravity 8.87 m/s2
    Temperature 738.15 *K
    Moon(s) None
  • Earth

    Earth

    Our only home. The Earth is quite unique in the way of it being the only planet in the solar system that supports life and the only one to have liquid water on its surface. It's also the only planet in the solar system whose name doesn't derive from Greek/Roman mythology.

    Mass 5.972 x 1024 kg
    Orbit Period 365 days (Sun)
    Radius 6371 km (0.0092 *R☉)
    Gravity 9.807 m/s2
    Temperature 288.15 *K (average)
    Moon(s) The Moon
  • Mars

    Mars

    The Roman God of War (also called Ares by the Greeks) is the name given to the fourth planet from the Sun because of its bloody red color. Did you know that Mars has the largest volcanoes in the solar system, with its largest one (Olympus Mons) being wide enough to cover the entire state of New Mexico?

    Mass 6.4185 x 1023 kg
    Orbit Period 687 days (Sun)
    Radius 3390 km (0.0049 *R☉)
    Gravity 3.711 m/s2
    Temperature 186.15 *K to 268.15 *K
    Moon(s) Phobos & Deimos
  • The Asteroid Belt

    The Asteroid Belt

    The Asteroid Belt is a region between Jupiter's and Mars's orbital areas. The four largest objects in this belt are Ceres, Vesta, Pallas, and Hygiea. Their masses combined makes up half of the whole belt's total mass. The belt is located 2.2 to 3.2 *(Au) from the sun.

    Mass 2.8 x 1021 kg to 3.2 x 1021 kg (total)
    Orbit Period (Sun, Jupiter & Mars)
    Radius 375000 km (combined) (0.54 *R☉)
    Gravity *varies* m/s2
    Temperature *varies* *K
    Moon(s) wat
  • Jupiter

    Jupiter

    Named after the God of Gods in Roman Mythology, Jupiter is the largest planet in the solar system. In fact, it is so large, that it has 2.5 times the mass of the all of the other planets combined, not to mention its 67 moons.

    Mass 1.898 x 1027 kg
    Orbit Period 11.8 years (Sun)
    Radius 69911 km (0.1 *R☉)
    Gravity 24.79 m/s2
    Temperature 24273.2 *K (core) 128.15 *K (clouds)
    Moon(s) Io, Europa, Ganymede, Callisto & 63 others.
  • Saturn

    Saturn

    Saturn was the most distant of the five planets known to the ancients. Being similar to Jupiter, Saturn is mostly made of hydrogen and helium. Though its rings are mostly made of chunks of ice and small amounts of carbonaceous dust. The rings are very stretchy (very = 120700 km) but are amazingly thin (amazingly = 20 m).

    Mass 5.683 x 1026 kg
    Orbit Period 29.4 years (Sun)
    Radius 58232 km (0.084 *R☉)
    Gravity 10.44 m/s2
    Temperature 95.15 *K
    Moon(s) Titan, Enceladus, Mimas, Dione & 58 others.
  • Uranus

    Uranus

    A gas & ice giant with no solid surface. The reason the planet appears blue is because of its atmosphere containing methane, which absorbs red light and allows blue and green hues to be reflected, giving the planet a beautiful greenish blue color.

    Mass 8.681 x 1025 kg
    Orbit Period 84 years (Sun)
    Radius 25362 km (0.036 *R☉)
    Gravity 8.69 m/s2
    Temperature 55.15 *K to 120.15 *K (troposphere)
    Moon(s) Miranda, Titania, Oberon, Ariel & 23 others.
  • Neptune

    Neptune

    This gas giant is the most dense one of them all and is believed to have formed much closer to the Sun in early solar system history before migrating to its current position. The Great Dark Spot can be found here, which consists of anticyclonic storms with a tremendous speed of 2400 km/h.

    Mass 1.024 x 1026 kg
    Orbit Period 164.8 years (Sun)
    Radius 24622 km (0.035 *R☉)
    Gravity 11.15 m/s2
    Temperature 72.15 *K (troposphere)
    Moon(s) Triton, Nereid, Despina, Thalassa & 10 others.
  • Pluto

    Pluto

    Pluto was once considered the 9th planet in the solar system, before it was reclassified to a dwarf planet when the International Astronomical Union described the definition of a planet. This dwarf planet is so small, that it has 66% of the diameter of the Earth's moon and 18% of its mass.

    Mass 1.309 x 1022 kg
    Orbit Period 247.68 years (Sun)
    Radius 1147 km (0.002 *R☉)
    Gravity 0.658 m/s2
    Temperature 33 *K to 55 *K
    Moon(s) Hydra, Nix, Charon & 2 others.

About.

This page was created as a voluntary project to include some of the basics of CSS, HTML and the tiniest bit of JavaScript & the jQuery library.
I decided to make the page about the solar system because of my interest in Astronomy and for the sake of teaching others about our own neighborhood of celestial bodies.
I chose this type of project not only to express my creativity but to show others that it's okay to choose your own type of project, as long as you include everything that you've learned (tip: including extra things will always give you better grades).

It all started with two circles; one big and yellow and the other one being little and blue, which were included in a project that CodeCademy (a site I highly recommend) suggested for new users. The idea was to create a very simple version of the solar system by making the blue circle orbit around the yellow one and at the same time having a grey circle orbiting around the blue one, which would represent the orbit of the Moon around the Earth and the Earth around the Sun.

*.

A table of definitions and usage of thingies that are marked with an asterisk (*).

Keyword Abbreviation for Description Use
Sgr A* Sagittarius A* A region in the middle of the Milky Way (our galaxy). It is known as a complex radio source as it emits strong radio waves (Infrared, X-ray and Gamma-ray) in outer space. It's believed that a supermassive blackhole lives there. Celestial bodies that orbit the center of the Milky Way
R☉ Solar Radius The solar radius is self-explanatory as its name reveals everything it's about. R☉ is a unit of length and its value is approximately 695 500 km (6.955 x 108 metres). Expressing the size of stars
Au Astronomical Unit A unit of length in which the exact value is 149 597 870 700 metres and is the mean distance between the Earth and the Sun. Measurement of long distances in outer space
K Kelvin An absolute thermodynamic temperature scale, where its minimum value (value of 0), is the temperature where all thermal motion ceases and where every atom is still. Unlike the Celsius and the Fahrenheit temperature scales, no negative values are present.
